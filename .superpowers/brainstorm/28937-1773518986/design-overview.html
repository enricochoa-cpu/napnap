6 Month Old Sleep Schedule
Wake windows, nap times, and bedtime for your 6-month-old

Total sleep: 14–15h
What to expect at 6 months
At six months, your baby is settling into a solid 3-nap routine. Wake windows stretch to about 2 hours between sleeps, and you'll notice a predictable rhythm forming. The third nap of the day is often a short "bridge" nap — just 30 minutes to help your baby make it to bedtime without getting overtired...

19:00Bedtime
Understanding wake windows
A wake window is the time your baby stays awake between sleeps. At 6 months, these stretch to about 2–2.5 hours. The first window of the day is usually the shortest (around 2 hours), while the last — from the final nap to bedtime — is the longest (up to 2.5 hours)...
Signs to watch for
• Fighting the third nap — Your baby may be getting ready to drop to 2 naps. Look for consistent refusal over 1–2 weeks before making the switch.
• Early morning wakes — If your baby is waking before 6am, bedtime might be too late. Try shifting it 15 minutes earlier.
• Short naps — 30-minute naps are developmentally normal. Most babies consolidate naps between 5–7 months.
